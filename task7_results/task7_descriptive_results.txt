As training length T (in cycles) increases:

- Training RMSE decreases consistently for all three ρ values,
following an approximate power-law:  RMSE ~ a · T^b

- Fitted exponents (all negative, confirming decay):
    ρ=25:  b = -0.179,  R² = 0.939
    ρ=28:  b = -0.174,  R² = 0.867
    ρ=31:  b = -0.159,  R² = 0.845

- The decay is sub-linear (b ≈ -0.17), much shallower than linear
(b=-1). Doubling T only reduces RMSE by ~11%  (2^(-0.17) ≈ 0.89),
showing strong diminishing returns.

- The exponent b is nearly identical across all three ρ values
(-0.159 to -0.179), suggesting the functional form does NOT
strongly depend on the bifurcation parameter when ρ values are
chosen within a similar dynamical regime (25-31).

- The prefactor a decreases with ρ (0.0117 → 0.0087 → 0.0079),
meaning higher ρ values have lower overall error scale within
this range.

- Practical saturation occurs around 800-900 cycles — beyond this
improvement is marginal due to finite reservoir capacity (N=200).
